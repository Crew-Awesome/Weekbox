# Privacy Policy

**Effective date: July 20, 2026**

WeekBox is a free, open-source desktop application for discovering, installing, and managing Friday Night Funkin' mods and engines. This policy explains the information WeekBox handles and the network services it contacts.

## Information stored on your device

WeekBox stores its settings and library information locally on your device. This can include your chosen storage location, download preferences, installed engine and mod metadata, and links to downloads you chose. WeekBox does not operate an account system or upload this local library information to a WeekBox-operated server.

## Network connections

WeekBox connects to third-party services when needed to provide features you use, including:

- GameBanana, to search for and display mod information, images, and download choices.
- GitHub, to check WeekBox releases and to retrieve release information when requested.
- Sniro / Psych Online, where its mod listings are available.
- Download hosts selected by you or by a release source, such as GameBanana, GitHub, Google Drive, and MediaFire, to download mods, engines, or an app release.
- Google Fonts and Font Awesome CDNs, to load the interface fonts and icons.

These services receive the technical information that is normally sent when your device makes a web request, such as your IP address, browser or WebView user agent, request time, and the requested URL. Their handling of that information is governed by their own privacy policies. WeekBox does not control those services.

## What WeekBox does not do

WeekBox does not intentionally collect or sell personal information, run advertising, use analytics or tracking SDKs, or require an account. It does not send your mod library, chosen storage path, or app settings to a WeekBox-operated server.

## Your choices

You can use locally installed content without an internet connection. Features that search for content, check releases, load remote images, or download files require a connection to the relevant third-party service. You can remove locally stored WeekBox data by deleting the folder you selected for WeekBox storage and uninstalling the application.

## Changes to this policy

If WeekBox changes how it handles information or adds a service that changes this policy, this document will be updated with a new effective date.

## Contact

For privacy questions or concerns, open an issue in the [WeekBox GitHub repository](https://github.com/Crew-Awesome/Weekbox/issues).

This policy describes WeekBox's current behavior and is not legal advice.
